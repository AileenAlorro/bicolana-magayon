The Proportionality Quest

Discover the math superpower that helps you scale recipes, read maps, and understand the world around you!

What Makes Something Proportional?

A relationship is proportional if the ratio between two quantities is always the same. If you double one thing, the other doubles too! Think of it as a "fair share" relationship. The key is the **constant of proportionality (k)**, which is the magic number that connects the two quantities.

Equation: y = kx

Spotting Proportionality in Tables

Proportionality in Graphs

A graph is proportional if it's a **straight line** that passes through the **origin (0,0)**.

Putting Proportions to Work

Once you know something is proportional, you can solve all kinds of problems, like finding the best deal at a store.

How to Find the Best Deal

Imagine 4 oranges cost $2.00. How much do 10 cost? Find the **unit rate** first!

1

Find the Unit Rate: Divide cost by quantity. $2.00 / 4 oranges = $0.50 per orange.

2

Solve: Multiply the unit rate by the new quantity. 10 oranges * $0.50 = $5.00.

Scaling Area: A Common Trap!

When you scale a shape's sides, its **area** scales by the factor **squared**!
